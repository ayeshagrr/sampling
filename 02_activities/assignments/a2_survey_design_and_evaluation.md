## Part B - Survey Evaluation:

Identify and describe survey features:

```         
Sample type: Cross-sectional, probability sample using a stratified design (stratification at the province/census metropolitan area level). Information is collected from one randomly selected household member aged 15 or older, and proxy responses are not permitted.

Sample size: Used a field sample of approximatively 50,000 units and invitation letters to the electronic questionnaire were sent to about 40,000 selected households across Canada. A completion of 24,000 questionnaires was expected and final overall response rate was 41.9%.

Target population: All persons 15 years of age and older living in the ten provinces of Canada. 

Sampling frame: A frame combining landline and cellular telephone numbers from Census and administrative sources plus Statistics Canadaâ€™s dwelling frame; records are telephone-number groups linked to an address when possible.

Survey mode(s): Electronic questionnaire or through CATI (computer assisted telephone interviewing).

Timeline: 2018-09-04 to 2018-12-28

Response rate: 41.9%.

Weights: Basic person weight for population estimates (WGHT_PER) and bootstrap weights  provided for design-based variance estimation.

Data processing: Data processed using Social Survey Processing Environment (SSPE). Micro and macro level checks including family, consistency, and flow edits.

Cleaning, imputation, etc: Item/partial nonresponse handled mainly using donor (nearest-neighbour) imputation via a score function; mean imputation used when donor imputation wasnâ€™t possible. Imputation carried out in nine steps, covering income, formal volunteering, informal volunteering, donation file variables, and solicitation method variables.

Sources of error:

Sampling error: because estimates come from a sample; variance estimated using the bootstrap method with bootstrap weights.

Non-sampling error: includes coverage error, nonresponse, response error, and processing error.

Coverage error source explicitly mentioned: exclusion of households without telephones / frame coverage gaps.

Limitations, known biases, etc:

Sampling error: survey is based on a sample rather than a full census, estimates are subject to random variation and may differ from the true population values.

Low response rate (41.9%): response rate below 50% increases the risk that respondents may differ systematically from non-respondents, potentially affecting representativeness.

Non-response bias: Non-response occurred at both the household and individual levels. Although survey weights were adjusted using administrative data, residual bias may remain if non-respondents differ in unmeasured ways.

Coverage error (telephone-based frame): Households without telephones or not covered by the telephone frame were excluded from the surveyed population, which may bias results if these households differ from those included.

Undercoverage and overcoverage: Despite improvements over past random digit dialing methods, the survey frame may still include some overcoverage or undercoverage relative to the target population.

Response error: Errors may arise from inaccurate or biased responses provided by participants (e.g., misunderstanding questions or recall issues).

Processing error: Errors may occur during data processing, coding, editing, or weighting, despite quality control procedures.
```
